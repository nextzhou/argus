# Code of Conduct

## Our Standards

We want Argus to be a professional, respectful, and useful project for everyone.
Participants in this community are expected to:

- Be respectful in language and behavior
- Assume good intent and discuss technical disagreements in good faith
- Focus feedback on code, design, and behavior rather than individuals
- Share context clearly when reporting bugs, proposing changes, or reviewing work
- Accept that maintainers may prioritize stability, scope control, and maintainability over feature breadth

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- Harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks
- Sexualized language or imagery
- Trolling, baiting, or deliberately disruptive behavior
- Publishing private information without permission
- Repeatedly ignoring maintainer decisions after they have been explained

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to project spaces, including issues, pull requests,
discussions, and other public interactions around the repository.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards.
They may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other
contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct.

To report a Code of Conduct issue, contact the maintainer at
`nextzhou@gmail.com`. Reports will be reviewed and handled as confidentially as
practical.
